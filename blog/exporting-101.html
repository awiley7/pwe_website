Exporting 101: Learn How to Start Exporting and Take Your Business to the Next Level

Posted: 2023-01-15

Are you ready to take your business global? Exporting your products can bring a whole new world of opportunities for your company! Not only can it increase your customer base and revenue, but it can also give your brand a boost of international prestige.

The benefits…

First things first, let's talk about the financial benefits. By exporting your products, you're opening up new markets and increasing your customer base. This means more sales and more revenue for your business. Plus, by diversifying your customer base, you're also reducing your dependence on domestic markets. This means that even if the economy in your home country starts tanking, your business will still be able to thrive in other markets. How are you going to know if your products become a big hit in other markets if you don't go global?

But exporting isn't just about the money, it's also about the prestige. Imagine having your products on shelves in countries all over the world! How cool would it be to tell your friends and family that your products are being sold in Japan, Australia, or even France? Not only will it make for some great conversation starters, but it can also give your brand a major boost of international credibility. Maybe you find out that your product is a big hit in Cape Town and that you want to expand more there. Also, people love international brands, so the international exposure of your products can help drive sales in your local market as well.

Another great benefit of exporting is the ability to learn from different cultures and markets. By exporting your products, you'll be exposed to new ways of doing business and new ways of thinking about your industry. This can lead to innovation and new ideas for your company. Plus, it can also help you to better understand and serve your customers, both at home and abroad. The world is too big to stay in just one place, so if you haven't already, exporting can give you new ambitions to travel, experience other cultures and see the world! You never know, maybe you can build a strong partnership with another international company that helps you scale your business.

Finally, exporting can also be a great way to create jobs and support economic growth. By increasing sales and revenue, your business will be able to expand and hire more employees. This not only benefits your company, but it also benefits your community and your country.

The risks to be aware of…

While exporting can bring a whole new world of opportunities for your company, it's important to be aware of the potential risks as well.

One major risk is the potential for cultural misunderstandings. Different countries have different customs, laws, and business practices. Without proper research and understanding, it can be easy to make mistakes that can damage your company's reputation or even lead to legal trouble. Also business etiquette changes from region to region, so make sure you are properly informed on how to engage your potential partners. Examples can be the use of direct eye contact in business meetings. Americans this is basically a given, but in Japan, it can be a sign of disrespect.

Another risk is the potential for currency fluctuations. The value of different currencies can change rapidly, and this can greatly affect the cost of exporting your products. This can make it difficult to set prices and can also lead to unexpected financial losses. I think we all experienced that in 2022 seeing the US Dollar surpass the EURO (It's called soccer now!).

Political instability in the country where you are exporting to can also be a huge risk, as it can affect the ability to import or export goods, or even the safety of your employees. This may be surprising to some, but when you start exporting to smaller and lesser developed countries, you need to be cautious of scams and corruption (yes it's a thing!).

Language barriers can also be a risk, as it can be difficult to communicate effectively with customers, suppliers, and partners in other countries. It pays to have an interpreter if you are working with a big deal (no Google Translate won't cut it!).

Finally, there is also a risk of intellectual property theft, as it can be difficult to protect your company's proprietary information, trademarks, and patents in other countries. No, the U.S. law does not supersede all other law, make sure you are aware that every country has their own laws and regulations. You may have a patent in the U.S., but that might not mean anything in Argentina or India.

Despite these risks, with the proper research and preparation, it's possible to mitigate them and take advantage of the many benefits exporting can offer. It's important to research the market, understand the laws and customs of the country you are exporting to, and consult with experts in international trade. Additionally, it's important to have a plan in place to handle unexpected challenges, such as currency fluctuations or political instability.

So, while exporting can bring a whole new world of opportunities for your company, it's important to be aware of the potential risks as well. But with the proper research and preparation, you can mitigate these risks and take advantage of the many benefits exporting can offer. Make sure to hire an experienced customs broker to help guide you through the process.

How to get started…

So, you're ready to take the leap and start exporting your products? Great! Here's a quick overview of the steps you'll need to take to get started.

  1. Research the market: The first step is to research the market you're interested in exporting to. This includes understanding the laws, customs, and business practices of the country, as well as identifying potential customers and competitors.
  2. Identify your target market: Once you have a good understanding of the market, you'll need to identify your target market. This includes understanding the needs and preferences of your potential customers and identifying the most effective channels for reaching them. Also look if that country has any trade agreements with the U.S., it could help save cost on duties and tariffs.
  3. Obtain the necessary licenses and permits: Exporting products often requires obtaining various licenses and permits. It's important to research the requirements for the country you're exporting to and obtain the necessary documentation. Did you know that cars exported to the Dominican Republic can't be older than 5 years? Sorry, that 1998 Chevy Astro van will have to stay in the U.S. for now.
  4. Find a freight forwarder and customs broker: These guys should be in your speed dial, right under your mom. You will need to find a freight forwarder or customs broker to handle the logistics of exporting your products. They will be able to handle all the details of shipping your products and navigating customs regulations. You can find plenty on https://www.cbp.gov/contact/find-broker-by-port.
  5. Develop a marketing strategy: Developing a strong marketing strategy is key to getting your products in front of potential customers. This includes identifying the most effective channels for reaching your target market, such as social media, email marketing, or trade shows. Take a look at what goods are in demand and see how you can find a niche. Don't just gamble on a container of fuzzy socks and hope they sell in Morocco.
  6. Prepare your team: It's important to prepare your team for the challenges of exporting. This includes providing training on the laws, customs, and business practices of the country you're exporting to, as well as preparing them to handle any potential challenges. If you are small or tight on funds, higher some help or better yet, us!

Exporting can be a complex process, but with the right research and preparation, it can also be a great opportunity for your business. Remember that it's important to consult with experts in international trade and have a plan in place to handle unexpected challenges.

By following these steps and being prepared, you'll be on your way to expanding your customer base, increasing your revenue, and taking your business to new heights. (Don't forget about us little guys when you make it big!)

The big finale…

So, are you ready to take your business global? Exporting your products can bring a whole new world of opportunities for your company. It's not just about the money, it's also about the prestige and the ability to learn from different cultures and markets. Plus, it can also be a great way to create jobs and support economic growth. So, what are you waiting for? Happy exporting!
